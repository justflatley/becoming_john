Becoming who I am

By John Flatley

On April 19, 2015

In General

Bantu tribesman, it is said, sneak into the rooms of their children as they sleep and whisper in their ears, “Become who you are.”

At the end of my Fundamentals of Business Thinking course in 2007, my professor, Jeff Sucec, gave us his book “Become who you are.” It isÂ a compilation of quotes and thoughts that have inspired him. On the first page is the quote above.

Recently, I decided to leave my job as a technical project manager at Google working for the Wallet risk team. I didn’t leave because I hated Google, who could with all the free food and perks? I didn’t leave because of the people, Google hires the best and brightest in the world. I left partly because of burnout, but I did that to myself. The bigger reason I left was because I didn’t feel like working in e-commerce was reflective of who I was.

Before I embark on my next career, whatever that may be, I decided I wanted to travel and experience the world. If you had asked me a few years ago if I would do something like this my answer would have been “No, I’m not a hippy.” Well, here I am going on my hippy adventures in some hope that I will get a better sense of who I am.
